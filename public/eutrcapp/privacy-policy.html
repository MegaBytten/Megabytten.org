Mega-privacy policy

Updated September 10th, 2022

This privacy policy describes how the MegaBytten network of software collects and uses your personal data. In the final sections of this privacy policy, I advise you on security warnings to consider when using and/or interacting with the MegaBytten software.

It is assumed that all users of MegaBytten software have read, understand and agree with the information in this policy prior to using any software.

If you have any questions regarding data usage, or the software itself, please feel free to contact me via email or discord

I strongly dislike any form of data sharing between companies and unessential data collection. Cookies - although powerful - have become a bane on everyday data-ownership, with most people unaware of all the data that websites obtain from users via cookies. With every piece of software I write, my aim is to ask strictly for the essential and functional data required for the software. Thus far I have been able to keep my website cookie free, giving users back all the power and ownership when it comes to their data.

Across all of my developed software, only the Touch Training Tracker (aliased as “EUTRC App”) requires data to function. More information on this app can be found at megabytten.org/eutrcapp. The app asks for the user’s first and last name, registered @exeter.ac.uk email address, phone number, and a password. Personal data is securely stored, and never shared with any third party companies, for any reason.

Note: Touch Training Tracker also referred to as by its development name, EUTRC App

Catering the app exclusively for Exeter University Touch Rugby Club members requires certain pieces of information. A registered @exeter.ac.uk email address being the most pivotal. Users can otherwise freely sign up with any first or last name, whether they be: nick-names, preferred names, names including personal pronouns, or anything else. The freedom across these categories exists to show users that their personal data is solely used for personalisation and functionality, and that users are ultimately in control of their data. Personal data is securely stored, and never shared with any third party companies, for any reason.

You have probably noticed when logging onto this site that your web browser displayed a warning that the site is not secure. This is because MegaBytten.org operates on HTTP, not HTTPS. HTTPS is a more recent, secure re-creation of how devices are able to communicate with each other through the internet (traditionally HTTP). By always encrypting the data that is transferred between devices, the data transferred by HTTPS is far more secure.

HTTP-transferred data is not always intercepted, it is still safe to use unless your device or network has been compromised. To learn more about HTTP vs HTTPS, visit this blog post.
